What is Personal Property Coverage for Renters?

Personal property coverage (also called "Contents Coverage" or "Coverage C" in renters insurance policies) reimburses you for the cost to repair or replace your personal belongings if they're damaged, destroyed, or stolen due to a covered peril.

Replacement Cost vs. Actual Cash Value

How your policy values your belongings makes a huge difference in what you receive after a claim:

Actual Cash Value (ACV):

  • Pays depreciated value of your belongings
  • Example: 3-year-old $1,500 laptop ‚Üí Pays ~$500
  • Example: 5-year-old $2,000 couch ‚Üí Pays ~$600
  • Lower premium but inadequate reimbursement
  • Leaves you severely short of replacement cost

Replacement Cost (RC):

  • Pays current cost to buy new equivalent items
  • Example: 3-year-old $1,500 laptop ‚Üí Pays $1,200 for new equivalent
  • Example: 5-year-old $2,000 couch ‚Üí Pays $2,200 for similar new couch
  • Allows you to actually replace your belongings
  • Only 10-20% higher premium than ACV

Creating a Home Inventory

After a total loss, you'll need to list every item you owned. A home inventory makes this much easier:

  1. Use Your Smartphone: Walk through your apartment recording video
  2. Narrate as You Go: Describe items and approximate values
  3. Open Drawers and Closets: Show clothing, shoes, accessories
  4. Photograph Receipts: For expensive items, keep digital receipt copies
  5. Use Inventory Apps: Sortly, Encircle, or "Know Your Stuff" app
  6. Store Safely: Upload video/photos to cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox)
  7. Update Regularly: Record new purchases, especially expensive items
  8. Share with Agent: Give Cloud Insurance a copy for safekeeping

‚ö†Ô∏è Without Documentation, Claims Are Difficult

After a total loss, you must prove what you owned:

‚Ä¢ Memory alone isn't enough
‚Ä¢ Insurance will ask for detailed lists with values
‚Ä¢ Without proof, they may question claimed items
‚Ä¢ Video/photo inventory speeds up claims dramatically
‚Ä¢ Takes 30 minutes now, saves months of frustration later

Filing a Personal Property Claim

If your belongings are damaged, destroyed, or stolen:

  1. Ensure Safety First: If fire or emergency, evacuate and call 911
  2. Contact Cloud Insurance: (973) 449-3671 as soon as possible
  3. Document Everything: Photos/video of all damage
  4. Don't Throw Away: Keep damaged items for adjuster inspection
  5. File Police Report: For theft/vandalism/burglary
  6. Make List: Every item damaged/stolen with approximate value
  7. Provide Documentation: Receipts, photos, credit card statements
  8. Prevent Further Damage: Board up, cover holes (save receipts‚Äîmay be reimbursed)
  9. Meet with Adjuster: Walk through with your inventory
  10. Be Patient: Claims take time, especially large losses
