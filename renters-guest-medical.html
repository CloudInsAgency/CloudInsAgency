Med Pay vs. Personal Liability: What's the Difference?

Many renters confuse these two coverages. Here's how they differ:

Feature Medical Payments (Med Pay) Personal Liability
Fault Required? NO - Pays regardless of fault YES - Only if you're legally liable
Typical Limit $1,000 - $5,000 per person $100,000 - $500,000+ total
Purpose Minor injuries, quick payment, goodwill Major injuries, lawsuits, legal defense
Legal Investigation? NO - Fast payment YES - Must determine liability
Legal Defense? NO - Voluntary payment YES - Attorney provided
Covers Pain & Suffering? NO - Medical bills only YES - All damages including pain & suffering
Speed FAST - Days/weeks SLOW - Months/years if lawsuit

What Guest Medical Payments Covers

‚úÖ Med Pay DOES Cover:

  • Medical expenses for guests injured in your apartment
  • Injuries on your property (apartment, balcony, hallway entrance)
  • Injuries from your activities anywhere (if your dog bites someone on walk)
  • All guests: Friends, family, neighbors, delivery people, anyone
  • Medical treatment: Doctor, ER, hospital, prescriptions
  • Dental injuries: Tooth damage from fall
  • Ambulance costs
  • Follow-up care related to injury
  • Treatment within 3 years of injury (typical time limit)

‚ùå Med Pay Does NOT Cover:

  • YOU (the renter): Your own injuries not covered
  • Household members: People living with you not covered
  • Regular tenants: Your roommates not covered
  • Intentional injuries: If you deliberately hurt someone
  • Business activities: Injuries related to business operations
  • Auto accidents: Covered by auto insurance
  • Workers: Contractors/workers have workers' comp

When to Use Med Pay

Med Pay is best for quick resolution of minor to moderate injuries:

Good Candidates for Med Pay:

  • Cuts requiring stitches ($500-$1,000)
  • Sprained ankle or wrist ($800-$1,500)
  • Minor head injury needing ER check ($1,500-$2,500)
  • Tooth chipped or broken ($1,000-$2,500)
  • Minor burns requiring treatment ($500-$1,500)
  • Injuries where fault is unclear
  • Guest is friend or family you want to help

When Med Pay May Not Be Enough:

  • Serious injuries requiring surgery (over $5,000)
  • Broken bones needing extensive treatment
  • Injuries causing permanent disability
  • Lost wages from injury
  • Guest pursuing pain and suffering claims
  • These situations need your liability coverage

Preventing Guest Injuries

While Med Pay provides great protection, prevention is always better:

  • Keep walkways clear: No clutter, cords, obstacles
  • Ensure adequate lighting: Especially stairs, bathroom
  • Secure loose rugs: Use non-slip pads underneath
  • Clean up spills immediately: Wet floors are slip hazards
  • Keep bathroom safe: Bath mats, grab bars if possible
  • Supervise children: Watch kids playing in your apartment
  • Control your pets: Restrain dogs around new people
  • Warn of hazards: Tell guests about any issues
  • Maintain apartment: Fix broken items, loose tiles, etc.
  • Monitor alcohol: At parties, ensure guests not over-served
