Actual Cash Value vs. Replacement Cost Coverage

Essex County condo owners have two options for how claims are paid:

Actual Cash Value (ACV):

  • Pays depreciated value of items
  • Example: 5-year-old sofa originally $2,000 ‚Üí Pays ~$800 after depreciation
  • Lower premium
  • Often leaves you short of replacement costs

Replacement Cost Coverage:

  • Pays full cost to replace items with new equivalents
  • Example: 5-year-old $2,000 sofa ‚Üí Pays $2,200 for new comparable sofa
  • Higher premium (typically 10-15% more)
  • Allows you to actually replace everything

üí° Cloud Insurance Recommendation: Choose Replacement Cost

For Essex County condo owners, replacement cost coverage is almost always worth the extra premium.

Why?

Creating a Home Inventory for Your Condo

Documenting your belongings BEFORE a loss makes claims much easier:

  1. Go Room by Room: Systematically document every room
  2. Take Photos/Videos: Walk through with your phone, open closets and cabinets
  3. List Major Items: Note brand, model, purchase date, approximate value
  4. Keep Receipts: Save receipts for significant purchases
  5. Use Apps: Many free home inventory apps available
  6. Store Securely: Keep inventory in cloud storage or offsite (not in condo)
  7. Update Regularly: Add new purchases, remove items you no longer own
  8. Appraise Valuables: Get professional appraisals for jewelry, art, collectibles

üì± Recommended Home Inventory Methods

Apps:

‚Ä¢ Sortly (free version available)

Filing a Personal Property Claim

If you experience a loss in your Essex County condo:

  1. Document the Damage: Take photos/videos of all damaged items before cleanup
  2. Make Temporary Repairs: Prevent further damage, save receipts
  3. Don't Throw Away Items: Adjuster may need to see them
  4. Contact Cloud Insurance: (973) 449-3671 - We'll guide you through the process
  5. File Police Report: For theft or vandalism
  6. Create Detailed Inventory: List every damaged/stolen item with description, age, value
  7. Provide Proof of Ownership: Receipts, photos, credit card statements
  8. Work with Adjuster: Be thorough, answer questions, provide documentation
  9. Track Expenses: Keep records of all costs related to the loss
  10. Follow Up: Stay in contact with your insurer until claim is settled
