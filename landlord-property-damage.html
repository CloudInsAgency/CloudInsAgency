How Much Property Damage Coverage Do You Need?

Your coverage amount should equal the cost to rebuild your rental property from the ground up‚ÄîNOT the market value or purchase price.

‚ö†Ô∏è Replacement Cost vs. Market Value

CRITICAL: Insure for REPLACEMENT COST, not market value!

Example: Newark 2-Family Home

Market Value: $450,000 (includes land value, location value)
Replacement Cost: $325,000 (actual cost to rebuild structure)

Why the difference?
‚Ä¢ Market value includes LAND (which doesn't burn down)
‚Ä¢ Location/neighborhood value (doesn't need to be rebuilt)
‚Ä¢ Current market conditions (irrelevant to rebuilding cost)

Insure for $325,000 replacement cost‚ÄîNOT $450,000 market value!

Overinsuring wastes money on unnecessary premiums. Underinsuring leaves you with massive out-of-pocket costs after a total loss.

Actual Cash Value vs. Replacement Cost Coverage

This is one of the most important decisions for landlords:

Actual Cash Value (ACV):

  • Pays depreciated value of damaged property
  • Example: 10-year-old roof damaged ‚Üí Pays 50% of replacement cost
  • Leaves you severely short of actual repair/replacement cost
  • Lower premium but inadequate coverage
  • You pay the difference out-of-pocket

Replacement Cost Coverage:

  • Pays current cost to repair/replace with new materials
  • Example: 10-year-old roof ‚Üí Pays full cost of new roof
  • Allows you to actually repair/rebuild property
  • 10-20% higher premium than ACV
  • Essential for protecting your investment

For landlord properties, ALWAYS choose Replacement Cost over Actual Cash Value!

Ordinance or Law Coverage

This often-overlooked coverage is crucial for older rental properties:

What is Ordinance or Law Coverage?

Covers the extra cost to rebuild to CURRENT building codes when repairing/rebuilding after a covered loss.

Why You Need It:

  • Building codes have changed since older properties were built
  • After major damage, must rebuild to CURRENT codes, not old codes
  • Can add 25-50%+ to rebuilding costs
  • Standard policies typically only include 10% ordinance coverage

Example: Bloomfield 1950s Rental Building Fire

  • Original construction: 1955 (built to 1955 codes)
  • Fire causes 60% damage, requires major reconstruction
  • Must upgrade: Electrical system, plumbing, insulation, HVAC, sprinklers, etc.
  • Basic rebuild cost: $200,000
  • Code upgrade costs: $75,000
  • Total needed: $275,000

Without adequate ordinance coverage: Policy pays $200,000, you're short $75,000!
With 50% ordinance coverage: Policy pays full $275,000!

‚ö†Ô∏è Older Properties Need Higher Ordinance Coverage

If your Essex County rental property was built before 1980, increase ordinance or law coverage!

‚Ä¢ Standard 10% is often insufficient
‚Ä¢ Increase to 25-50% of dwelling coverage
‚Ä¢ Especially important for pre-1970 buildings
‚Ä¢ Code upgrades can easily exceed $50,000-$100,000
‚Ä¢ Cost to add: Usually $50-$200/year more

  • Higher premiums due to more exposure
  • More tenants = higher risk of claims
  • Ensure adequate coverage for shared systems

Section 8 / Subsidized Housing:

Short-Term Rentals (Airbnb, VRBO):

  • Standard landlord policies typically EXCLUDE short-term rentals
  • Need special short-term rental endorsement

  • Must disclose short-term rental to insurer
  • Coverage denied if not disclosed

Filing a Property Damage Claim

If your rental property suffers damage:

  1. Ensure Everyone is Safe: Tenants evacuated, no immediate danger
  2. Contact Cloud Insurance Immediately: (973) 449-3671 - Report claim ASAP
  3. Document All Damage: Extensive photos and video
  4. Prevent Further Damage: Board up, tarp roof, shut off water if needed
  5. Notify Tenants: Keep them informed about repairs timeline
  6. Save All Receipts: Emergency repairs may be reimbursed
  7. Don't Make Permanent Repairs: Wait for adjuster unless emergency
  8. Meet with Adjuster: Walk through damage, provide documentation
  9. Get Repair Estimates: Multiple bids from contractors
  10. Keep Records: All communications, estimates, invoices
