What is Medical Payments Coverage (Coverage F)?

Medical Payments coverage, officially called Coverage F in your homeowners insurance policy, pays reasonable medical expenses for people accidentally injured on your property‚Äîregardless of who is at fault. This is a no-fault coverage, meaning you don't have to be legally liable for the injury.

üè• How Coverage F Works in Essex County

Standard Coverage Amounts: $1,000 - $5,000 per person

Most Common: $1,000 (minimum) or $5,000 (recommended)

Cost Difference: Only $10-30 per year between $1,000 and $5,000

Key Advantage: No deductible applies to Coverage F‚Äîfull amount available for medical bills.

Payment Trigger: Injury must occur within 3 years of the accident, and person cannot be a regular resident of your home.

üí° How They Work Together

Medical Payments is first-line defense. When someone gets hurt on your property:

Step 1: Offer to pay medical bills through Coverage F ($1,000-$5,000)

Common Questions About Coverage F in Essex County

Q: If I pay medical bills through Coverage F, am I admitting fault?

A: No. Medical Payments coverage is "no-fault" coverage. Payment doesn't mean you were legally responsible. It's a goodwill gesture that your policy allows regardless of who was at fault.

Q: Can the injured person still sue me after accepting Coverage F payment?

A: Legally, yes‚Äîbut it's unlikely for small injuries. Most people are satisfied when their medical bills are paid and don't pursue further action. For larger injuries that exceed your Coverage F limit, they may pursue a liability claim for the excess.

Q: Does my dog's breed affect Coverage F?

A: Coverage F itself covers injuries from any dog. However, some carriers restrict or exclude certain breeds entirely from the policy, which would affect both Coverage F and E.

Q: What if someone gets hurt at my Essex County vacation home?

A: If the vacation home is covered on your homeowners or separate dwelling policy, Coverage F applies there too.

Q: Do I need this if I have umbrella insurance?

A: Yes! Umbrella insurance only kicks in after your underlying liability coverage is exhausted. Coverage F works independently and pays small claims immediately without triggering your umbrella or liability coverage.
