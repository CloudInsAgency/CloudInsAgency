New Jersey Eviction Process and Costs

Understanding the eviction process in NJ helps you appreciate why legal expense coverage is valuable:

NJ Eviction Timeline:

  • Notice Period: 30 days (month-to-month) or per lease terms
  • Court Filing: File complaint with Superior Court
  • Tenant Response: Tenant has time to answer (often contests)
  • Court Hearing: Scheduled, often 2-6 weeks out
  • Judgment: If landlord wins, judgment for possession issued
  • Warrant for Removal: Must obtain warrant
  • Sheriff Eviction: Sheriff physically removes tenant
  • Total Time: 2-4 months (uncontested) to 6-12 months (contested)

NJ Eviction Costs Breakdown:

  • Attorney fees: $1,500 - $5,000+ (depending on complexity)
  • Court filing fee: $50 - $275
  • Service of process: $50 - $150
  • Sheriff eviction fee: $150 - $300
  • Locksmith (change locks): $100 - $300
  • Storage of tenant belongings: $200 - $500
  • Cleaning and repairs: $500 - $5,000+

Fair Housing Legal Risks

Fair housing complaints are increasingly common and VERY expensive to defend:

Protected Classes Under Fair Housing:

  • Race, color, national origin
  • Religion
  • Sex (including sexual harassment, gender identity)
  • Familial status (children, pregnancy)
  • Disability (physical or mental)
  • Age (in some NJ jurisdictions)
  • Source of income (Section 8 - in some NJ areas)

Common Fair Housing Complaints:

  • Discriminatory screening or denial
  • Refusing reasonable accommodations (disability)
  • Different terms/conditions based on protected class
  • Steering applicants to certain units
  • Discriminatory advertising language
  • Refusing families with children
  • Refusing Section 8 vouchers (in covered municipalities)

Defense Costs:

  • Simple complaint defense: $5,000 - $15,000
  • Complex case with hearing: $15,000 - $50,000
  • Litigation if escalated: $50,000 - $200,000+
  • Potential damages if found liable: $25,000 - $500,000+

Tips for Reducing Legal Risks

While coverage protects you financially, prevention is always better:

Proper Tenant Screening:

  • Consistent screening criteria for all applicants
  • Background checks, credit checks, employment verification
  • Check previous landlord references
  • Document all screening decisions
  • Never screen based on protected classes

Clear Lease Agreements:

  • Use comprehensive, NJ-compliant lease
  • Clearly state all terms and conditions
  • Document tenant acknowledgment
  • Include all policies (pets, smoking, guests, etc.)
  • Have attorney review your lease

Document Everything:

  • Move-in condition reports with photos
  • All communication with tenants in writing
  • Maintenance requests and completion
  • Lease violations and warnings
  • Payment history
  • Move-out inspection reports

Follow Proper Procedures:

  • Give required notices (30-day, etc.)
  • Follow exact eviction procedures
  • Never "self-help" evict (change locks, shut utilities)
  • Return security deposits properly (within 30 days)
  • Provide itemized deduction lists
  • Follow lead paint disclosure laws

Fair Housing Compliance:

  • Train yourself on fair housing laws
  • Use neutral, objective screening criteria
  • Never ask about protected characteristics
  • Provide reasonable accommodations when required
  • Keep consistent policies for all tenants
  • Avoid discriminatory advertising language
