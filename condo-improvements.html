Understanding Your HOA Master Policy and Improvements

The type of HOA master policy determines what improvements are your responsibility:

"Bare Walls-In" (Most Common):

  • HOA covers: Building structure, exterior, common areas
  • You cover: EVERYTHING inside including all original AND improvements
  • Example: Original builder-grade kitchen + Your $30K renovation = You insure ALL of it
  • Needs: Comprehensive interior coverage including improvements

"All-In" (Single Entity):

  • HOA covers: Building AND all original fixtures and finishes
  • You cover: ONLY improvements beyond original
  • Example: Original kitchen covered by HOA, but YOUR $30K renovation is your responsibility
  • Needs: Coverage specifically for your improvements only

"Bare Walls-Out" (Rare):

  • HOA covers: Only exterior structure
  • You cover: Interior walls AND everything inside
  • Needs: Highest coverage required

üìã Example: How Master Policy Type Affects Your Coverage

Scenario: West Orange Condo with $35,000 Kitchen Renovation

Original builder kitchen value: $15,000

WITH "Bare Walls-In" Master Policy:
‚Ä¢ HOA covers: $0 of kitchen
‚Ä¢ You must insure: Original $15K + Renovation $35K = $50,000 total

WITH "All-In" Master Policy:
‚Ä¢ HOA covers: Original $15K kitchen
‚Ä¢ You must insure: Only your $35K renovation

Same renovation, different coverage needs based on master policy!

Replacement Cost vs. Actual Cash Value for Improvements

How your improvements are valued in a claim matters significantly:

Actual Cash Value (ACV):

  • Pays depreciated value of improvements
  • Example: $40K kitchen installed 5 years ago ‚Üí Pays ~$28K
  • Leaves you short of actual replacement cost
  • Lower premium but inadequate protection

Replacement Cost:

  • Pays current cost to replace improvements
  • Example: $40K kitchen 5 years ago ‚Üí Pays $48K (current cost)
  • Allows you to actually rebuild as it was
  • Slightly higher premium (10-15% more) but much better

üí° Cloud Insurance Strongly Recommends Replacement Cost

For improvements and renovations, replacement cost coverage is essential!

Your $50K kitchen renovation will cost $50K+ to replace. Actual cash value might only pay $35K after depreciation, leaving you $15K+ short.

The modest premium increase (10-15%) is absolutely worth it to fully protect your investment.
