What Earthquake Structural Coverage Does NOT Include

‚ùå Typically NOT Covered:

  • Land damage: Earth itself (sinkholes, landslides unless caused by earthquake)
  • Landscaping: Trees, shrubs, lawns
  • Detached structures (sometimes): Sheds, fences may have lower limits or exclusions
  • Exterior masonry: Some policies limit brick veneer, chimneys to specific amounts
  • Pre-existing damage: Cracks/damage that existed before earthquake
  • Settling/subsidence: Unless directly caused by earthquake
  • Flood damage: If earthquake causes dam failure (need flood insurance)
  • Fire damage: If earthquake causes fire (homeowners insurance covers fire)

Older Homes vs. Newer Construction

Older Homes (Pre-1960) - HIGHER RISK:

  • Not built to any seismic standards
  • Brick/masonry construction very vulnerable
  • Unreinforced foundations
  • Older mortar deteriorates over time
  • Chimneys often unbraced
  • Essex County has MANY older homes ‚Üí Higher risk!

Newer Homes (Post-2000) - MODERATE RISK:

  • Better construction methods
  • Wood frame more flexible
  • Modern foundations more resistant
  • Still vulnerable to moderate/large quakes
  • Not built to California seismic standards

How Earthquake Claims Work

  1. Earthquake Occurs: Seismic event damages your home
  2. Ensure Safety: Evacuate if unsafe, turn off utilities if needed
  3. Document Damage: Photos/video of all structural damage
  4. Contact Cloud Insurance: (973) 449-3671 - Report claim immediately
  5. Emergency Repairs: Cover broken windows, tarp roof to prevent further damage
  6. Adjuster Inspection: Insurance sends structural engineer/adjuster
  7. Damage Assessment: Engineer evaluates extent of structural damage
  8. Claim Estimate: Insurer provides repair/rebuild estimate
  9. Deductible Applied: Remember: 10-20% deductible (not $1,000-$2,500 like homeowners)
  10. Payment: Insurance pays for repairs above deductible
  11. Repairs Completed: Hire contractors to repair structural damage

üí° Important Claim Considerations

Timing: Most policies have a 72-hour waiting period after purchase (can't buy during/after quake)
Proof of Earthquake: USGS must have recorded seismic event
Multiple Aftershocks: Usually considered one event for deductible purposes
Engineering Reports: May require structural engineer assessment
Repair Timeline: Structural repairs can take 3-12 months
Living Expenses: If home uninhabitable, additional living expense coverage helps (separate from structural)
