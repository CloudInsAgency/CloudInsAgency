What is Loss Assessment Coverage?

Loss assessment coverage (also called "Coverage D - Loss Assessment" in HO-6 policies) reimburses you for special assessments charged by your condo association when the building's master insurance policy doesn't cover all the costs of a covered loss.

How Loss Assessments Work

Understanding the assessment process helps you see why coverage is essential:

  1. Building Experiences Covered Loss: Fire, storm, water damage, etc.
  2. HOA Files Claim: Submits claim to master insurance policy
  3. Insurance Pays: Master policy pays up to limits, minus deductible
  4. Shortfall Identified: HOA calculates uncovered costs
  5. Assessment Voted: HOA board votes on special assessment
  6. Owners Notified: You receive assessment notice with amount due
  7. Payment Due: Typically 30-60 days to pay
  8. Your Insurance Responds: You file claim with your HO-6 policy
  9. Reimbursement: Your loss assessment coverage pays (up to your limit)

What Loss Assessment Coverage Includes and Excludes

‚úÖ Loss Assessment Coverage DOES Cover:

  • Master policy deductibles divided among owners
  • Damage exceeding master policy limits
  • Covered perils that damage common property:
    • Fire and smoke damage
    • Wind and hail damage
    • Water damage (non-flood)
    • Vandalism
    • Theft of HOA property
  • Liability assessments (if HOA is sued and loses)
  • Emergency repairs to prevent further damage

‚ùå Loss Assessment Coverage Does NOT Cover:

  • Regular HOA fees or monthly dues
  • Planned capital improvements (elevator upgrades, etc.)
  • Maintenance assessments (painting, landscaping)
  • Earthquake damage (unless you have earthquake endorsement)
  • Flood damage (unless you have flood endorsement)
  • Assessments from excluded perils
  • Fines or penalties from HOA

What to Do If Your HOA Charges a Loss Assessment

If your condo association sends you a special assessment notice:

  1. Read the Notice Carefully: Understand what the assessment is for
  2. Verify It's a Covered Loss: Make sure it's due to sudden damage, not planned maintenance
  3. Contact Cloud Insurance Immediately: (973) 449-3671
  4. File a Claim: Submit claim to your HO-6 insurance company
  5. Provide Documentation: Copy of assessment notice, HOA master policy claim info
  6. Keep All Records: Assessment notice, payment receipt, correspondence
  7. Pay the Assessment: Pay by the due date (your insurance will reimburse you)
  8. Submit Reimbursement Claim: Provide proof of payment to your insurer
  9. Receive Reimbursement: Your HO-6 policy pays you back (up to your limit)
